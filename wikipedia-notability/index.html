Could You Get a Wikipedia Page?

Answer 8 questions to assess your likelihood of meeting Wikipedia's notability guidelines

Before You Start

Wikipedia has strict notability requirements. Most people and companies do not qualify. This is not a personal judgment. It reflects Wikipedia's mission to be an encyclopedia of verifiable, notable subjects rather than a directory of everyone and everything.

The core requirement is simple but demanding: significant coverage in multiple reliable, independent sources.

What counts:

  • Feature articles, profiles, or in-depth coverage in newspapers, magazines, or books
  • Coverage from multiple unrelated publications (not syndicated content)
  • Sources with editorial oversight and fact-checking

What does not count:

  • Press releases (even if republished by news sites)
  • Your own website, social media, or marketing materials
  • Brief mentions, directory listings, or event announcements
  • Interviews where you talk about yourself

Q1
What type of subject is this Wikipedia page for?
Companies and commercial entities face stricter standards than individuals
Q2
How many independent publications have written about this subject at length?
Count only publications unaffiliated with the subject. Multiple articles from the same publication count as one.
Q3
What is the highest quality source that has covered this subject?
Consider the most prestigious/reliable publication, not the most recent
Q4
What type of coverage has the subject received?
Wikipedia requires "significant coverage" meaning in-depth treatment, not just mentions
Q5
What is the geographic reach of the coverage?
For companies especially, Wikipedia requires coverage beyond local media
Q6
How long has the subject been receiving media coverage?
Sustained coverage over time is stronger than a recent burst of attention
Q7
Has the subject received notable awards, recognition, or achieved significant milestones?
Major industry awards, academic honors, or record achievements can establish presumed notability
Q8
Are the sources truly independent of the subject?
Press releases republished by media do not count. Paid placements do not count. Interviews where the subject promotes themselves do not count.
